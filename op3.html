Broiled or baked fish recipe

Broiled or baked fish can do?

Learning tips by Dietary plan

Broiled or baked fish can be a beneficial option for people with Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) because it is a low-potassium and low-phosphorus protein source. Fish is also a good source of omega-3 fatty acids which can help to lower inflammation in the body, lower blood pressure and improve overall cardiovascular health.

Ingredients

  • 4 orange roughy, red snapper, catfish or trout fillets (6 ounces each)
  • 6 tablespoons butter, melted, divided
  • 1 tablespoon all-purpose flour
  • Paprika
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 1 tablespoon minced fresh parsley
  • 2 teaspoons Worcestershire sauce


    • How to Prepare Peppered Chicken with Garlic and Honey

  • Place fish on a broiler rack that has been coated with cooking spray.
  • Drizzle 3 tablespoons butter over fillets.
  • Dust with flour and sprinkle with paprika.
  • Broil 5-6 in. from the heat for 5 minutes or until fish just begins to brown
  • Combine lemon juice, parsley
  • Worcestershire sauce and remaining butter
  • Pour over fish.
  • Broil 5 minutes longer or until fish flakes easily with a fork.


    • Nutrition Facts

    (per serving)

  • Calories ----------------- 292
  • Carbohydrates -------- 3g
  • Protein ------------------ 28g
  • Dietary Fiber ----------- 0g
  • Total Fat ---------------- 18g
  • Sodium ----------------- 40mg
  • Potassium -------------- 144mg
  • Phosphorus ------------ 99mg
