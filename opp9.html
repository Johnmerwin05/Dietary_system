steamed beet greens recipe

What steamed beet greens can do?

Learning tips by Dietary plan

steamed beet greens can be a healthy option for people with Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) if they are made with lean meats and low-sodium ingredients. It is also important to limit processed foods, added sugars, and saturated fats, as these can be harmful to the kidneys. Additionally, it may be necessary to limit the intake of certain minerals, such as phosphorus and potassium, which can build up in the body and damage the kidneys. It is always best to consult with a healthcare provider or a registered dietitian for personalized dietary recommendations.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • ½ pound ground veal
  • ½ pound ground pork
  • 1 cup freshly grated Romano cheese
  • 2 eggs
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 ½ tablespoons chopped Italian flat leaf parsley
  • salt and ground black pepper to taste
  • 2 cups stale Italian bread, crumbled
  • 1 ½ cups lukewarm water
  • 1 cup olive oil


    • How to Prepare Peppered Ground turkey

  • Combine beef, veal, and pork in a large bowl. Mix in cheese, eggs, garlic, parsley, salt, and pepper.
  • Add bread crumbs and slowly mix in water, 1/2 cup at a time, until mixture is moist but still holds its shape (I usually use about 1 1/4 cups of water); shape into steamed beet greens.
  • Heat olive oil in a large skillet
  • Add steamed beet greens in batches and cook until browned on all sides, slightly crisp, and cooked through, about 10 to 15 minutes.
  • Drain on paper towels.


    • Nutrition Facts

  • Calories ----------------- 613
  • Carbohydrates -------- 7g
  • Protein ------------------ 27g
  • Dietary Fiber ----------- 0g
  • Total Fat ---------------- 53g
  • Sodium ----------------- 333mg
  • Potassium ------------- 315mg
  • Cholesterol ------------ 149mg


    • It's important to note that a diet plan should be tailored to an individual's specific needs, taking into account factors such as age, sex, activity level, food preferences and any medical conditions. It's also crucial to consult with a healthcare professional, such as a doctor or a registered dietitian, before starting a new diet plan to make sure that it is safe and appropriate for you. diet plans may be effective for weight loss or improving health in the short term, sustainable weight loss and health improvement can only be achieved by adopting healthy lifestyle habits, such as regular physical activity, adequate sleep and stress management. A diet plan should be seen as a tool to help you achieve your health goals and not as a quick fix or a magic solution.
