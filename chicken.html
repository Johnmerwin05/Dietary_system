Food recommendation Grilled or baked lean cuts of beef
Meats Dishes by Dietary plan

Grilled or baked lean cuts of beef can be a good option for people with chronic kidney disease (CKD) because they are lower in potassium, phosphorus and salt. It is important to note that people with CKD should limit their overall intake of red meat and it's also important to prepare the meat in a healthy way, such as grilling or baking, rather than frying.

Food recommendation Broiled or baked fish
Meats Dishes by Dietary plan

Broiled or baked fish can be a good option for people with chronic kidney disease (CKD) because it is a low-potassium and low-phosphorus protein source. It's also important to note that people with CKD should limit their intake of high-mercury fish such as swordfish, tilefish, king mackerel, and shark. When preparing fish, it's best to bake or broil it to retain its nutritional value and avoid adding extra sodium or unhealthy fats.

Food recommendation Chicken breast
Meats Dishes by Dietary plan

Chicken breast is a lean, low-fat source of protein that can be a beneficial part of a diet for people with Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD). Eating chicken breast as part of a well-balanced diet can help Maintain muscle mass, Low in phosphorus and potassium , Lower risk of cardiovascular disease, Helps with weight management and Provides energy.

Food recommendation Tenderloin or sirloin steak
Meats Dishes by Dietary plan

Tenderloin or sirloin steak can be a good option for people with chronic kidney disease (CKD) because they are lean cuts of beef, which means they are lower in potassium, phosphorus and sodium. Tenderloin, also known as filet mignon, is the most tender cut of beef and is considered a lean cut with less than 10 grams of total fat and 4 grams of saturated fat per 3-ounce serving.
