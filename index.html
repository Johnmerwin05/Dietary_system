About us What is CKD?
Learning tips by Dietary plan

Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) is a condition in which the kidneys gradually lose function over time. This can lead to a build-up of waste products and excess fluid in the body, and can increase the risk of health problems such as heart disease and stroke. The condition is typically classified into five stages, based on the level of kidney function, with Stage 1 being the mildest and Stage 5 being the most severe (end-stage renal disease). The progression of CKD can be slowed or even stopped if it is detected early and managed properly.

Diet Plans What Diet Plan can do?
Learning tips by Dietary plan

A diet plan for people with Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) typically involves certain dietary restrictions to help slow the progression of the condition and manage symptoms. The specifics of the diet plan will depend on the stage of the disease and any other medical conditions the person may have. A registered dietitian can help create an individualized meal plan that is tailored to the specific needs of the person and their stage of CKD. it is important for patients to work closely with their healthcare provider and dietitian to manage this condition effectively.

Food Catagories Chicken breast
Meats Dishes by Dietary plan

Chicken breast is a lean, low-fat source of protein that can be a beneficial part of a diet for people with Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD). Eating chicken breast as part of a well-balanced diet can help Maintain muscle mass, Low in phosphorus and potassium , Lower risk of cardiovascular disease, Helps with weight management and Provides energy.

Vegetable Dishes by Dietary plan

Cabbage is a versatile and nutritious vegetable that can be a beneficial part of a diet for people with Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD). Eating cabbage as part of a well-balanced diet can help Low in Potassium and Phosphorus, High in Vitamin C and antioxidants, Anti-inflammatory properties, Promote healthy digestion and Help manage blood pressure.

Food Catagories Milk
Drinks dishes By Dietary plan

Milk is a good source of calcium, protein, and other essential nutrients, but it is high in potassium and phosphorus, so it's important to limit the intake or choose low-potassium and low-phosphorus options such as skim milk, or lactose-free milk. Milk is a nutritious drink that can provide many benefits for people with Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD), but it's important to be mindful of its potassium and phosphorus content.
